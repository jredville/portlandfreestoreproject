# Portland Free Store Project — Website Redesign Proposal

**Prepared for:** Portland Free Store Project Team  
**Date:** July 2025  
**Author:** Jim Deville

---

## Summary

The Portland Free Store Project is doing meaningful, community-rooted work — and the website should reflect that. The store is beloved, vibrant, and mission-driven, but the current site is sparse and doesn't tell the full story of what makes it special.

This proposal outlines what's missing, what a redesigned version looks like, and a clear path to make it happen — **staying on Squarespace** with a 50% nonprofit discount applied first.

A preview of the new design is available at:  
👉 **[https://jredville.github.io/portlandfreestoreproject/](https://jredville.github.io/portlandfreestoreproject/)**

---

## What's Working on the Current Site

- Org name and welcome message are in place
- Email signup is present on the homepage
- Instagram and TikTok links are in the footer
- FAQ, About, Store Policies, Donate, and Contact pages exist
- The address and EIN are in the footer

---

## What's Missing or Needs Improvement

**Things visitors need but can't easily find:**

- **A compelling homepage** — the current home page has a welcome sentence and a signup form, but no photos, no carousel, no strong visual identity, and no clear call to action
- **Store hours** — the hours are buried or not prominently visible; they should be front and center and displayed as real text (not an image), so they show up in Google searches
- **A donate page that shows the fundraising goal** — the current donate page lists amounts and Venmo but doesn't communicate the monthly goal or progress. "50 monthly subscriptions of $20 each would pay our rent" is a compelling story that should be front and center
- **The merch section needs more visibility** — the hand-printed shirts by Evie Butler are a great community touchpoint and should be easier to find
- **A contact form** — the contact page currently only gives an email address; a simple form reduces friction for people who want to reach out

**Things that could be clearer:**

- **The cart icon in the navigation** implies e-commerce, which is the opposite of the free store's message — it should be removed
- **The lavender color palette** is pleasant but doesn't convey the warm, community-rooted feel of the space
- **The "Donate Here!" label in navigation** — this works, but "Donate" is cleaner and less pressured
- **The About page origin story** — it's there, but it deserves more prominence; the story of seeing that TikTok and deciding to build something like it in Portland is a great hook

## The Plan: Stay on Squarespace, Cut the Cost in Half

### Step 1 — Get the Nonprofit Discount (Do This First)

Squarespace offers a **50% discount** for verified 501(c)(3) nonprofits through TechSoup. This is free to apply for and takes about 1–5 business days.

**How to get it:**

1. **Gather your documents** — you'll need EIN 33-1296139 and your IRS determination letter (the letter confirming 501(c)(3) status).

2. **Create a TechSoup account** — go to [techsoup.org](https://www.techsoup.org) and register as a nonprofit. TechSoup verifies eligibility and issues software discount tokens at no cost.

3. **Request the Squarespace offer** — once verified, find Squarespace in TechSoup's offer catalog and request a discount token.

4. **Apply the discount in Squarespace** — go to your Squarespace account → **Settings → Billing** → enter the TechSoup token.

**What it costs after the discount:**

| Plan | Regular price | After 50% discount |
|---|---|---|
| Personal | ~$16/mo | ~$8/mo |
| Business | ~$23/mo | ~$12/mo |

The Business plan is recommended — it allows custom code blocks for TikTok embeds, a fundraising thermometer widget, and Squarespace's built-in email campaigns.

### Step 2 — Port the New Design to Squarespace

Once the team has reviewed the preview site and signed off, Jim will rebuild the site in Squarespace using the new design — same pages, same content, same structure — but native to the Squarespace editor so the team can maintain it without any technical help.

**What gets built:**
- All 6 pages (Home, About, FAQ, Store Policies, Donate, Contact)
- New earth tone color palette via Squarespace's style editor
- Real-text store hours front and center
- Cart icon removed from navigation
- Contact form using Squarespace's built-in form block
- Embedded Google Map on the Contact page
- TikTok embed and Instagram link on the Home page
- Email signup connected to the existing list
- Donate page with thermometer, Venmo, merch section, and donor thank-yous

### Step 3 — Hand Off for Easy Ongoing Maintenance

All updates happen through the Squarespace visual editor — no code, no technical knowledge required. Any volunteer can:

- Update store hours by clicking the text and typing new times
- Add or edit FAQ items with the block editor
- Update donation amounts or the fundraising goal
- Swap in new photos for the carousel
- Edit any text on any page

## Alternative Option: Custom Hosting on Fly.io

For the team's awareness, there is a lower-cost alternative to Squarespace — hosting the custom-designed site on **Fly.io**, a modern on-demand hosting platform. This option is included here for transparency, not as the primary recommendation.

### How It Works

Rather than using Squarespace's editor, the site would be hosted as a static HTML/CSS/JS website (like the preview you're looking at now) on Fly.io's infrastructure. Fly.io spins up a small server only when someone visits the site, which keeps costs very low.

### Estimated Cost

| Item | Monthly Cost |
|---|---|
| Fly.io hosting (shared CPU, 256MB RAM) | ~$0–$2/mo |
| Custom domain (portlandfreestoreproject.org) | ~$1.50/mo (already have this) |
| **Total** | **Under $5/month** |

Fly.io has a free tier that covers low-traffic nonprofit sites most months — you'd only pay if traffic spikes, and even then it stays well under $5/month.

### The Trade-Off

The catch is that **this option requires working with someone who knows HTML and web technology** to make any content changes — updating hours, adding team photos, editing the FAQ, etc. There's no visual editor like Squarespace.

Jim is happy to continue being that person and to work with the team on updates as needed. If the team is comfortable with that arrangement, this is the most affordable path. If the team wants to be able to make changes independently without any technical help, Squarespace is the better fit.

### Side-by-Side Comparison

| | Squarespace (with TechSoup discount) | Fly.io |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly cost | ~$12/mo (Business plan) | Under $5/mo |
| Content updates | Any volunteer, no tech needed | Requires HTML/tech help |
| Features | Full Squarespace editor, built-in forms, email campaigns | Whatever we build — full control |
| Maintenance | Squarespace manages the platform | Jim manages updates |
| Best for | Team independence | Lowest possible cost |
